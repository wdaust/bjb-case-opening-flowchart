Liability Profile Scoring System Defense Pressure & Settlement Leverage Model Purpose This model measures: 1. How difficult it is for the defense to escape fault 2. How predictable a jury outcome would be 3. How expensive and risky it is for the carrier to fight When measured consistently, these scores become powerful predictors of: settlement timing offer quality litigation necessity attorney resource allocation STRUCTURE OF THE MODEL We produce TWO SCORES, not one. Liability Strength Index (LSI) Pure fault + proof. Settlement Leverage Index (SLI) LSI + coverage + venue + defense exposure + readiness. This prevents confusion between: “Is liability strong?” vs “Will they pay?” CATEGORY SCORING WITH RUBRICS (1–5) Each category must be scored using objective anchors. Fault Clarity (Weight: 25%) Can the defense reasonably dispute responsibility? **5** – Clear statutory violation OR admission + no adverse facts **4** – Strong police support + minor disputes **3** – Balanced versions, arguable both ways **2** – Real comp neg exposure **1** – Defense narrative stronger 2) Evidence Strength (20%) Objective, permanent proof. **5** – Video/body cam + photos + consistency **3** – Some documentation but gaps **1** – Testimonial only / preservation problems 3) Witness Profile (10%) **5** – Neutral independent + secured **3** – Passenger/related **1** – None or hostile 4) Police / Official Documentation (10%) **5** – Supports client, diagram + notes **3** – Neutral **1** – Unfavorable or weak 5) Comparative Negligence Exposure (10%) **5** – None **3** – Possible arguments **1** – Serious shared fault risk Settlement Leverage Add-Ons These influence payment behavior, not fault itself. 6) Coverage & Collectability (10%) **5** – Limits known, umbrella, employer **3** – Standard coverage **1** – Asset or policy issues 7) Defendant / Jury Optics (5%) **5** – Commercial / bad actor **1** – Highly sympathetic defendant 8) Defense Escape Routes (5%) How many ways can they fight? Includes: * Causation gaps * Delay in treatment * Prior injury * Recorded statements * Social media * SIU posture **5** – Very limited **1** – Many viable attacks 9) Documentation Readiness (5%) Can we present immediately? **5** – Organized, authenticated, demand-ready **1** – Disorganized, missing items WEIGHTED OUTPUTS ### Liability Strength Index (LSI) Categories 1–5 → weighted → score out of 100. ### Settlement Leverage Index (SLI) LSI + categories 6–9 → score out of 100. DATA HYGIENE GATE (CRITICAL) No score can be considered final unless minimum information exists. Track **Liability Score Completeness %**: Required inputs: ✔ Police report status ✔ Citation/admission ✔ Witness status ✔ Photo/video existence ✔ Coverage verification ✔ Comp neg review If missing → system shows **PROVISIONAL**. AUTOMATIC ACTION TRIGGERS 80–100 Early limits strategy Leadership visibility Rapid demand 60–79 Normal demand cycle Evidence strengthening 40–59 Liability development sprint Supervisor review <40 Early litigation consult Client expectation management REASON CODES (EXECUTIVE GOLD) Every score should display: • Top Strength Drivers o Example: citation + video • Top Weakness Drivers o Example: treatment delay • Most Valuable Missing Item o Example: independent witness statement GOVERNANCE & OWNERSHIP To prevent opinion scoring: | Stage | Who Scores | | Intake | Provisional | | Early pre-lit | Case manager update | | Pre-demand | Attorney validation | | New evidence | Mandatory re-score | WHAT LEADERSHIP SEES Instead of anecdotes, they get: • which cases should settle fast • which deserve premium push • which need more development • which are trending toward litigation • where attorneys should spend time WHY THIS IS ELITE Because it is: • Standardized • evidence-based • weighted • repeatable • auditable • operationally actionable Not vibes. Policy & Collectability Scoring System Transportation No insurance, auto or health - will it work to give a letter of protection (LOP) Recovery Power & Payment Predictability Model Purpose This system measures: • How much insurance money is realistically available • How difficult it will be to access it • How motivated the carrier is to pay • Whether litigation will be required to unlock value This predicts: • ceiling of recovery • negotiation leverage • reserve behavior • likelihood of tender • time to settlement STRUCTURE OF THE MODEL Again — we produce TWO SCORES. Coverage Strength Index (CSI) How good the available coverage is. Recovery Difficulty Index (RDI) How hard it will be to extract payment. Because: • High limits + impossible carrier = frustration • Moderate limits + easy carrier = fast money CATEGORY SCORING (1–5 RUBRICS) Objective anchors. No guessing. 1) Policy Limits Adequacy (Weight: 25%) Are limits appropriate relative to damages? 5 – Clearly insufficient vs exposure (pressure to tender) 4 – Near likely value 3 – Might be tight 2 – Plenty of room 1 – Excessively high / low urgency 2) Coverage Verification Status (15%) How certain are we? 5 – Dec page or written confirmation 4 – Carrier verbally confirmed 3 – Likely but not documented 2 – Disputed 1 – Unknown 3) Additional Policy Potential (10%) Stacking opportunity. Umbrella Employer Vehicle owner Commercial Additional insured 5 – Strong multi-layer 1 – Single small policy 4) Carrier Tender Behavior (10%) Historical behavior. 5 – Known tender carrier 3 – Mixed 1 – Chronic fighters 5) Defendant Collectability Outside Insurance (5%) Assets, business backing, etc. Recovery Difficulty Add-Ons These influence how painful payment will be. 6) Reservation of Rights / Coverage Defenses (10%) 5 – None 1 – Serious issues 7) UM/UIM Complexity (10%) Offsets, exhaustion, consent issues. 8) Lien & Subrogation Pressure (5%) High liens = reduced net → negotiation complications. 9) Multi-Party Allocation Risk (5%) More claimants = less predictability. 10) Documentation Readiness (5%) Proof of coverage demand-ready? WEIGHTED OUTPUTS Coverage Strength Index (CSI) How big the money bucket is. Recovery Difficulty Index (RDI) How hard it will be to reach into it. Treatment Strength Index (TSI) A structured way to rate the **quality, seriousness, credibility, and profitability** of medical treatment. You can use 1–5 scales, color ratings, or weighted points. Body parts are experiencing pain Pain levels New injury or an excacerbated injury Fracture or a scar The 5 Master Treatment Scores (Leadership View) 1. **Medical Severity** 2. **Treatment Consistency** 3. **Provider Credibility** 4. **Objective Proof** 5. **Future Exposure** From these, you can instantly see: ✔ High-value files ✔ Jury-worthy cases ✔ Soft or defensive cases ✔ Cases needing development ✔ Where adjusters will fight ✔ Where they will fold --- --- # Comprehensive Case Performance Index (CPI) “How strong is this file as a business asset?” This index measures how efficiently, profitably, and strategically viable the case is — independent of liability, policy, treatment, or client personality. The 7 Pillars of the Case Performance Index Each scored 0–20 Total = 0–140 Converted to 0–100 if preferred Economic Efficiency Score (Is this case economically viable relative to internal cost?) This measures the operational cost-to-value relationship. Score Factors: Staff hours invested to date Estimated future labor hours Vendor costs incurred Lien complexity burden Case age relative to value band Projected cost-to-resolution ratio High Score: ✔ Low labor relative to expected fee ✔ Clean file, limited administrative drain Low Score: ✖ High labor drain ✖ Complex admin or lien burden ✖ Aging with unclear upside This protects margin. Momentum & Velocity Score (Is this case moving at the proper operational speed?) This is not treatment scoring. It measures system flow. Score Factors: Time in current stage vs benchmark Time since last meaningful milestone Stage regression events Number of reopenings or rework loops Idle days without forward movement Bottleneck frequency High Score: ✔ Smooth stage progression ✔ No rework ✔ Minimal stagnation Low Score: ✖ Repeated task reopening ✖ Files drifting ✖ Chronic delays This predicts inventory health. File Integrity & Organization Score (How structurally clean is the file?) Not data hygiene — structural readiness. Score Factors: Required documents present Internal summaries up to date Chronology built Evidence cataloged Attachments properly indexed Demand materials partially staged No “search hunting” required High Score: ✔ Clean, professional file ✔ Easy for claims or lit to absorb Low Score: ✖ Disorganized ✖ Missing summaries ✖ Hard to interpret quickly This reduces friction between departments. Defense Friction Index (How difficult will this case be to settle?) This is not liability strength — it measures resistance probability. Score Factors: Carrier reputation for delay Prior defense posture trends Adjuster responsiveness history SIU involvement signals Recorded statement issues Early denials or partial denials Defense counsel aggressiveness (if known) High Score: ✔ Cooperative carrier ✔ Predictable adjuster Low Score: ✖ SIU heavy ✖ Known stall tactics ✖ Aggressive defense strategy This predicts negotiation time and litigation conversion likelihood. Strategic Priority Score (Does this case matter beyond dollars?) This is portfolio strategy modeling. Score Factors: Case type firm wants to expand Venue expansion importance Carrier pattern testing opportunity Precedent-building value Marketing synergy potential Referral source importance Relationship value High Score: ✔ Strategic case ✔ Aligns with firm growth targets Low Score: ✖ Isolated or non-strategic This helps allocate attorney time intelligently. Risk Exposure Score (What internal risk does this case carry?) Not liability — operational/legal risk. Score Factors: Statute proximity Missed internal deadlines Compliance exposure Client complaint probability Ethical complexity flags Record gaps that could create malpractice exposure Supervisory audit flags High Score: ✔ Clean compliance posture Low Score: ✖ Deadline compression ✖ Prior missed obligations ✖ Documentation weakness This is your early-warning system.
